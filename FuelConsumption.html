How This Fuel Consumption Calculator Works

Most online tools hide the formulas behind the calculator interface. Here are the transparent equations:

Metric Formula (L/100 km)

L/100 km = (litres used ÷ kilometres driven) × 100

Divide the litres you poured in by the kilometres you just drove, then multiply by 100. The answer tells you how many litres your car needs to travel 100 kilometres.

Imperial Formula (MPG)

MPG = miles ÷ gallons

Divide the miles you drove by the gallons you added. The answer tells you how many miles your car can travel on one gallon of fuel.

Cost per Kilometre Formula

Cost/km = (litres used ÷ km) × price per litre

Divide litres by kilometres, then multiply by the price you paid per litre. The answer is the exact cost of driving one kilometre – perfect for splitting bills with friends or invoicing clients.

Example

→Distance: 120 km
→Fuel used: 8.5 L
→Fuel price: 1.85 zł/L

Step 1: L/100 km = (8.5 ÷ 120) × 100 = 7.08 L/100 km
Step 2: Total trip cost = 8.5 × 1.85 = 15.73 zł
Step 3: Cost per km = 15.73 ÷ 120 = 0.13 zł/km

This calculation shows more than just numbers on a screen. It tells you that your car consumes about 7 litres of fuel per 100 km, which is a clear indicator of efficiency. At the same time, you see the true cost of your journey in this case, 15.73 zł for 120 km. Breaking it down further, each kilometre costs you 0.13 zł, a figure that can be very useful when planning your monthly fuel budget, splitting costs with passengers, or comparing the running expense of different cars.

Where Standard Calculators Stop

Most basic calculators focus only on single trips. They often ignore important real-world situations such as:

Missing Factor Typical Calculator Real-world Use Case Cost Error if Ignored
Car-pooling Not included Several people share fuel cost Entire trip appears more expensive
Daily commute Treated as one trip Small daily drives repeated 250+ times a year Thousands of kilometres uncounted
Return journey Often one-way only Most trips include return distance Costs underestimated by ~50%
Driving efficiency Not included Fuel-efficient driving reduces usage Missed savings opportunities

Without these adjustments, you may underestimate your true fuel expenses significantly.

Eco-Driving Tips

Adopting efficient driving habits can significantly reduce fuel consumption.

Practical Checklist

→ Check tyre pressure: Slightly higher pressure can cut consumption by 2–3%.
→ Maintain steady speed: Driving at 50–60 km/h in top gear is often most efficient.
→ Limit air conditioning use: Turning it off below highway speeds can save ~5%.
→ Anticipate braking: Gentle deceleration saves energy.
→ Remove unnecessary weight: Roof racks, boxes, or heavy items increase drag and consumption.
→ Plan routes wisely: Avoid unnecessary detours and peak-hour traffic when possible.

By combining a calculator with these tips, you can see and measure improvements directly.

FAQs

Q1. My car shows L/100 km on the dashboard – why use a calculator?

Because a calculator adds trip cost, annual projections, and per-person splits, which dashboards don’t provide.

Q2. Can this be used for electric vehicles?

Yes. Replace litres with kWh used. The formula remains the same, and cost per kilometre can still be calculated.

Q3. Does it work in heavy traffic?

Yes. Fuel used while idling is included in your refill. Divide litres by distance and the result accounts for traffic conditions automatically.

Q4. How accurate is it?

Within about ±3%, provided you refill consistently to the same level and reset your trip meter.

Q5. Can I embed this tool online?

Yes. A simple widget can be added to websites, blogs, or personal dashboards for live calculation.
