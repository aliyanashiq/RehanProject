How This Day Calculator Works

The calculator computes the number of days between two dates by subtracting the start date from the end date. If you want to include both start and end dates, you add 1 to the result.

Total Days = (End Date − Start Date) + 1

To calculate business days (excluding weekends and public holidays), the calculator subtracts the number of weekend days and public holidays:

Business Days = Total Days − Weekend Days − Public Holidays

This formula allows you to understand how the calculator counts days transparently and precisely.

How It Works in Practice

→The calculator simply subtracts the earlier date from the later date.

→If you tick “inclusive”, it adds 1 to count both start and end.

→For business days, it scans the calendar and skips Saturdays, Sundays, and any holidays you pick.

Inside the Engine – How the Calculator Really Counts

When you click “Calculate,” the tool doesn’t just flip through a paper calendar. Instead, it uses a precise internal method based on Unix timestamps.

How It Works

→Unix timestamps represent dates as milliseconds since January 1, 1970.

→The calculator converts both start and end dates into these timestamps.

→It subtracts the earlier timestamp from the later one to get total milliseconds.

→Dividing by 86,400,000 gives the raw count of days.

Business Days Calculation

To count business days, the engine iterates through every date in the range, flags Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays, and subtracts them from the total.

Real-life Edge Case Example

Start: 29 February 2024 (leap year, Thursday)
End: 1 March 2024 (Friday)
Raw count: 2 days
Business-day count: 2 days (both weekdays)
Leap year is automatically detected through Unix timestamps.

Business Days vs. Calendar Days – Why the Gap Matters

A 30-day span may include 8–10 weekend days plus holidays, meaning 25–30% of days are non-working. The calculator shows both numbers instantly:

→Calendar days: Total elapsed time including weekends and holidays.

→Business days: Working days excluding weekends and public holidays.

Real-life Example

You sign a contract on 3 June 2024 with a 90-business-day completion clause.
Raw calendar math: 90 days ≈ 3 months.
Weekends and holidays add 25 non-working days.
Actual deadline: 115 calendar days later – 3 September 2024.
